Trip report IETF87 (Berlin 2013-07-28--08-02)

This IETF was more hallway track and ad-hoc meetings than ordinary
working group sessions for me. Highlights during the week included

- informal meeting regarding [[http://policyreview.info/articles/news/ietf-%25E2%2580%2593-berlin-meeting-did-not-overlook-mass-surveillance/186][mass surveillance]], coinciding with the
  publishing of [[http://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/jul/31/nsa-top-secret-program-online-data][XKEYSCORE]]

- jacob appelbaum and i [[https://www.ietf.org/proceedings/87/slides/slides-87-saag-5.pdf][presenting tor]] at SAAG

- discussing [[http://www.tschofenig.priv.at/wp/?p%3D993][tor and the ietf]] and what they can bring to each other
  with hannes tschofenig and stephen farrell, among others

- planning libradsec for moonshot with josh howlett, sam hartman,
  stefan winter, rhys smith

- discussing ephemeral keys in abfab with sam hartman

- radext (OPS) about DTLS, NAI normalisation, dynamic discovery and
  fragmentation

- meeting with numerous others, mostly on tor and/or security
